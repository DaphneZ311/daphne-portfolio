Multidimensional Bayesian Causal Inference in Perception

MATLAB Psychophysics Modeling

The Question: How does the brain integrate conflicting multisensory signals (Audio/Visual) across time and number?

Working with Dr. Ladan Shams, I implemented and fitted computational models extending the Sound-Induced Flash Illusion. Contribution: We proved that human perception follows a "Multidimensional" Bayesian inference process. This theoretical work underpins my belief that mental health issues often stem from mismatches between sensory evidence and internal predictions.

Adaptive Vibrotactile Stimulation for Real-Time Stress Modulation

Python/R LME Models Biopac (ECG/GSR)

The Question: Can we modulate user stress levels using haptic feedback, and does the user's expectation change the physiological outcome?

I designed a counterbalanced experiment (N=40) recording 7,000+ datapoints of HRV, GSR, and PPG during cognitive load tasks. Key Finding: Vibration is not a static "relaxer." It reduced subjective arousal only when aligned with user predictions (priors), confirming a Bayesian mechanism in haptic perception. This suggests future digital health tools must be context-aware and expectation-sensitive.

Aesthetic Chills: Emotional Peaks as Signals for Adaptive Systems

Quant Research (N=110) Performance Modeling Qualtrics/JS

The Question: "Chills" (frisson) represent moments of high prediction error. How do these emotional peaks reorganize memory and time perception?

Leading a team of 5, I conducted a large-scale study analyzing how physiological chills trade off episodic memory detail for temporal coherence. Implication for HCI: Emotional inflection points are detectable signatures that systems can use to time interventions—pacing changes or grounding prompts—during moments of cognitive vulnerability.

Lead Researcher (Modeling)

Sequential Grouping & The Chinese Restaurant Process

Computational Modeling Psychophysics MATLAB

The Question: Does the brain integrate sensory signals in parallel (as classic models suggest), or does it group them sequentially based on arrival time?

I applied the Chinese Restaurant Process (CRP)—a stochastic process from Machine Learning—to model human perception, validating it with a complex 3-stimulus spatial task. Key Finding: We discovered a distinct "Order Effect." Once an early cue forms a reliable cluster, it biases how later cues are perceived. This suggests the brain constructs structured groups over time rather than integrating all signals blindly.

Inference Under Uncertainty in Large Language Models

Python (PyTorch) Transformers NLP

The Question: Do artificial neural networks face the same integration challenges as biological brains when processing syntax vs. semantics?

I analyzed attention heads in GPT-2 and BERT, computing "surprisal" values to test if syntactic processing is encapsulated. Key Finding: Attention heads thought to encode "pure" syntax actually degrade when semantic plausibility is violated. This suggests LLMs perform joint probabilistic inference, much like humans, rather than processing structure and meaning independently.

Research Philosophy

Technology no longer just extends human capability; it shapes our cognitive and emotional rhythms.

My research bridges Cognitive Science and HCI. I believe that to build effective digital mental health tools, we cannot just design better screens; we must design systems that understand the user's internal model.

By treating attention and emotion as adaptive resources rather than static constraints, I aim to create interfaces that act as cognitive partners.
